VENUS CYCLE
Venus in its apparent Geocentric cycle through the zodiac moves through all 12 signs in one year
until it Retrogrades, which it does every 18 months.  Venus will stay Retrograde for approximately
6-weeks.  As with any retrograde and direct movement, always pay attention to  the degrees of the
STATIONS, and how this will aspect the natal chart and which house or houses are transited.
Heliocentric orbital cycle is 225 days.  Return Cycle to conjunction within 3 degrees of the same sign, is
7.997 years. This is only two days, eight hours short of exactly eight years.
MARS CYCLE
Mars
Retrograde
Mars retrogrades about every year and 11.3 months, with approximately
712 days between the time Mars goes Direct and the next time it appears
Retrograde.  Retrograde means “moving backwards,” something Mars
appears to be doing to the observer on Earth, though Mars is “not” actually
moving backwards but continuing on in its 687 day orbit around the Sun.
Because the Earth only takes 365
days to go around the Sun it
eventually passes Mars, catching up
with Mars every 23.3 months.
Even though Mars is not really
moving backwards, it can be said that
these retrograde periods do mark an
important “phenomenon” in the
orbital relationships between the
Earth, Mars and the Sun.  As the
Earth approaches within 40 days of
passing Mars, Mars will appear to the
observer on Earth to stop and slowly
reverse its relative position in the sky
over the course of the next 80 days.
Forty days into this retrograde period
Mars, Earth and the Sun will all be in
an alignment that astrologers call a
Sun-Mars opposition.
The astrological meaning of a
retrograde Mars is very simple in
theory.  Because Mars is associated
with our natural urge to take action,

or to assert ourselves aggressively, a retrograde Mars concentrates this force in a more
specific range.  Each retrograde Mars period is in a different Sign of the Zodiac and thus
concentrates this energy in different ways for each of us every time.  Though we are all
affected by a retrograde Mars, the degree to which it affects us depends upon how the
planets were positioned when you were born. Serious students of astrology will know
where Mars is retrograding in their natal or birth chart.
Whatever house Mars retrogrades in will be a hot area of activity.  In the positive sense,
this means that a lot can get done in that area of your life!  In the negative sense, this
might imply friction or agitated situations that threaten or challenge you to take action.
Managing one’s anger and aggressiveness is very important in whatever area Mars is
retrograding in your natal chart, especially if it is in hard aspect to your ASC, MC, Sun,
Moon, Mercury, Venus or Mars.  The “proper use of force” is important to remember
during this retrograde period.  I have seen many clients make significant accomplishments
during a retrograde Mars.
For example, a retrograde Mars in your 4th House might signal a renovation of your home
or apartment or even moving.  Thus if Mars is retrograding through your 4th house, after
the retrograde you can be proud of what you’ve accomplished with domestic projects.
However, such projects will often take more time and more effort than was realized in the
beginning, and this is the downside of the retrograde.  I remember one case of a client in
New York City renovating a new loft apartment during a retrograde Mars.  During
renovation he discovered structural problems with the ceiling and support beams that
required far more work and expense to remedy than he had originally anticipated.
A more challenging example would be that of someone who has their Natal Moon in the 7th
house while Mars retrograded in their 4th house.  In such a case roommate situations could
become uncomfortable, or someone may move-in “temporarily” disrupting the harmony of
the environment.  With Mars squaring a 7th house Moon circumstances might also involve
a boyfriend/girlfriend situation or husband/wife issue that is upsetting the household.  Yet
even when dealing with upsets and challenges a retrograde Mars represents times to learn
how to handle one’s anger and direct one’s energy more appropriately and effectively.

Cycle Length: 11.88 years. Notes: (Check for exact dates.) Jupiter returns occur just

NODAL CYCLE
Cycle Length: 18.5997 years. Notes: The nodal axis returns to its original position at about
ages 37, 55, 74, and 95.

METONIC CYCLE
Cycle Length: 19 years. Notes: 235 synodic months = 254 sidereal months 19 years,
accurate within less than eighteen hours. Thus these solar and lunar rhythms return to
their natal starting points within days of the 19th, 38th, 57th, 76th  and 95th birthdays.
PROGRESSED LUNAR CYCLE
Cycle Length: 27.3 years. Notes: A progressed lunar return occurs at age 27, age 54, and
just short of age 82.
SATURN CYCLE
Cycle Length: 29.42 years. Notes: (Check for exact dates.) Saturn returns occur at age 29,
age 59, and age 88
CHIRON CYCLE
Cycle Length: 50.42 years. Notes: We experience a Chiron return at age 50 and at about
age 101.
URANUS CYCLE
Cycle Length: 83.75 years. Notes: Check for exact dates for waxing Uranian square
(average age 21), Uranian opposition (average age 42), and waning Uranian square
(average age 63). The Uranian return occurs just before the 84th birthday.
NEPTUNE CYCLE
Cycle Length: 163.72 years. Notes: (Check for exact dates.) Neptune square Neptune
oscillates between approximately ages 39 and 42. The Neptune opposition occurs in the
early eighties.
PLUTO CYCLE
Cycle Length: 248.6 years. Notes: (Check for exact dates.) Pluto square Pluto oscillates
between the late thirties and about age 90, marking one of the most distinct
psychological differences among generations.
