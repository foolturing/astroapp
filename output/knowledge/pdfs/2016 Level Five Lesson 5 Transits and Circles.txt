MARS CYCLE
Mars
Retrograde (April 17, 2016 at 20:15 Beijing Time until June 30 at 07:40 Beijing Time)
Yes, Mars is retrograde again, something that happens about every year and 11.3 months, with
approximately 712 days between the time Mars goes Direct and the next time it appears
Retrograde.  The last time Mars was retrograde was between March 2, 2014 and May 20, 2014
in the Sign of Libra.  This time Mars is retrograding in the Signs of Sagittarius and Scorpio.
Retrograde means “moving backwards,” something Mars appears to be doing to the observer
on Earth, though Mars is “not” actually
moving backwards but continuing on in its
687 day orbit around the Sun.  Because
the Earth only takes 365 days to go
around the Sun it eventually passes Mars,
catching up with Mars every 23.3 months.
Even though Mars is not really moving
backwards, it can be said that these
retrograde periods do mark an important
“phenomenon” in the orbital relationships
between the Earth, Mars and the Sun.  As
the Earth approaches within 40 days of
passing Mars, Mars will appear to the
observer on Earth to stop and slowly
reverse its relative position in the sky over
the course of the next 80 days. Forty days
into this retrograde period Mars, Earth
and the Sun will all be in an alignment
that astrologers call a Sun-Mars
opposition.  The earth is in between Mars
and the Sun, so from the Earth’s
perspective Mars and the Sun are on
exactly opposite sides of the Earth.  Every
retrograde Mars period is characterized
by this opposition, represented by the letter “d” in the diagram on the left.  This time the
opposition occurs on May 22, 2016 at 19:18 Beijing Time.  The Sun will be at 1 degree of the
Gemini and Mars at 1 degree of the Sagittarius.
The astrological meaning of a retrograde Mars is very simple in theory.  Because Mars is associated with
our natural urge to take action, or to assert ourselves aggressively, a retrograde Mars concentrates this
force in a more specific range.  Each retrograde Mars period is in a different Sign of the Zodiac and thus
concentrates this energy in different ways for each of us every time.  Though we are all affected by a
retrograde Mars, the degree to which it affects us depends upon how the planets were positioned when
you were born.  This Retrograde Mars occurs between 8 degrees of Sagittarius and 23 degrees of
Scorpio.  If you were born with the Sun, Moon, Ascendant or planets in any of the mutable signs Gemini,
Virgo, Sagittarius and Pisces, or fixed signs Taurus, Leo, Scorpio and Aquarius within the range of these
degrees then you will be more impacted by this retrograde Mars then those who were not.  Serious

students of astrology will know where Mars is retrograding in their natal or birth chart.  Whatever house
Mars retrogrades in will be a hot area of activity.  In the positive sense, this means that a lot can get
done in that area of your life!  In the negative sense, this might imply friction or agitated situations that
threaten or challenge you to take action.  Managing one’s anger and aggressiveness is very important in
whatever area Mars is retrograding in your natal chart, especially if it is in hard aspect to your ASC, MC,
Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus or Mars.  The “proper use of force” is important to remember during this
retrograde period.  I have seen many clients make significant accomplishments during a retrograde Mars.
For example, a retrograde Mars in your 4th House might signal a renovation of your home or apartment
or even moving.  Thus if Mars is retrograding through your 4th house, after the retrograde you can be
proud of what you’ve accomplished with domestic projects.  However, such projects will often take
more time and more effort than was realized in the beginning, and this is the downside of the
retrograde.  I remember one case of a client in New York City renovating a new loft apartment during a
retrograde Mars.  During renovation he discovered structural problems with the ceiling and support
beams that required far more work and expense to remedy than he had originally anticipated.  A more
challenging example would be that of someone who has their Natal Moon in the 7th house while Mars
retrograded in their 4th house.  In such a case roommate situations could become uncomfortable, or
someone may move-in “temporarily” disrupting the harmony of the environment.  With Mars squaring a
7th house Moon circumstances might also involve a boyfriend/girlfriend situation or husband/wife issue
that is upsetting the household.  Yet even when dealing with upsets and challenges a retrograde Mars
represents times to learn how to handle one’s anger and direct one’s energy more appropriately and
effectively.
