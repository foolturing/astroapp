Male
This is the natal chart of a long time client.  He makes his living as a landscape
architect in California.  He has lead a very dysfunctional and often, for him,
confusing relationship life.  Engaged once, he has a son from one former girlfriend
– who left him as soon as she was pregnant.  He pays child-support and is a
responsible weekend father twice a month – involved in his son’s life.  Over the last
14 years he has lived with 5 different women.  The following 3 charts are solar
return charts.  See if you can identify the key issues for each of these 3 years of
his life.
4 / 7
In this year he moved to a new apartment near the ocean with a new
girlfriend that was very supportive of him, and he was quite contented
and happy with her for this first year of living together.
5 / 7
In this year, 1999 – 2000, his fiancé broke off their engagement, and
he ended a business partnership with an architect that had been
somewhat of a mentor to him.  Both partnerships were tumultuous.
6 / 7
In this year 2010 – 2011, he made great progress in his professional life, receiving an international
award for his work, and a full color photo feature article in one of the leading architectural
magazines.  However, his live in girlfriend developed a severe drug and alcohol problem, during which
time she nearly burned down their apartment.  Her psychological problems, magnified by addiction,
required many months of hospitalization and led to the end of their relationship in 2011.
7 / 7
The Moon was in the 4th at birth and now is in the 10th, so career is highlighted this month.
However, the Moon is square Neptune, implying some confusion regarding career direction or
decisions in this area.  The ASC of the Lunar Return Chart is conjunct the natal Sun, making this a
significant month in terms of decisions or issue that impact partnership and work (ruler of natal 7th,
and in the natal 6th house).  The strong emphasis in the 9th house of the Lunar Return implies that
either education or travel are big themes that month.  Either work relate travel issues, or
educational developments that impact career and reputation and have a corresponding affect upon
their partnership and work life.  [In this month his PhD dissertation finally got the okay from the
faculty board, though it means he can only work part-time and must depend upon his wife’s financial
support while he works to complete his dissertation.]
