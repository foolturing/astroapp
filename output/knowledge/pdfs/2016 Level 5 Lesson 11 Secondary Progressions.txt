A PROGRESSION is simply one of many forecasting techniques that an astrologer uses to
advance or “progress” a chart forward for any time period in question.  There are different
types of progressions or “directions.”  The most common progressions are called Secondary
Progressions which are the progressions you will learn about first.

Secondary Progressions are called “secondary” to
distinguish them from “Primary Directions”.

Secondary Progressions are often called “day for a year”
progressions,  because they equate each day that follows birth with one
whole year of life.

Thus 24 hours in the Ephemeris following birth is equal to
12 months of life: 24 Ephemeris hours = 12 calendar months.
“What is the historical background of Secondary Progressions?”
Secondary Progressions were probably developed by the time of Ptolemy, as
Progressions were used by Arab astrologers in the last millennium A.D. in one
form or another.  You can consult.  [Devore, Op. Cit., P. 121] .  Nicholas
Devore, Encyclopedia of Astrology (New York: Philosophical Library, 1947) P.
346]  Also, Rob Hand offers an introduction on Secondary Progressions in
Robert Blaschke’s, Astrology, A Language of Life, Vol. I – Progressions.
“The whole idea of Secondary Progressions seems crazy to me.  How
can 1 day correspond to a whole year of someone’s life?”
The first time I was introduced to Secondary Progressions I felt the

correlated to real life experiences, which – as it turned out – they
did! Nonetheless I gave a lot of thought over the years to the theory
of Secondary Progressions, which I explain in the following pages.

Theory of Secondary Progressions:
 Early cosmic weather may establish a pattern duplicated in longer cycles
At first it may seem very strange to equate the hours and days following birth with the months
and years of a person’s life.  The question that arises is: “How can a smaller unit of time
correspond or correlate with a larger period of time?”  However, remember, we are
talking about patterns here.  During the first 90 to 120 days of an infant’s life, an infant is
no longer protected by the mineral rich embryonic fluid of the womb, and is now exposed
to an atmosphere of cosmic weather, i.e. a unique rhythm of solar, lunar and planetary
patterns.  This all begins with the first breath that sets the whole natal chart into motion.
Consider that there are also 2 so called “soft spots” on a baby’s head; one is called the
anterior fontanel, which fuses between 12 to 14 months, and the other is the posterior
fontanel, on the back part of the top of the head. It should be completely gone (because
the bones have fused) by four months.  The suggestion being that this posterior fontanel
may play a role in how these secondary progressions are set. Once established, these
secondary progressions operate as a kind of “internal” solar system, like ‘time-release
capsules’ that resonate with “real time” issues, experiences and circumstances.
 Progressions are like “Fractals
A fractal is a mathematical set described by Fractal Geometry, the study of figures exhibiting
fractal dimension. A fractal set when plotted typically displays self-similar patterns, which
means they are "the same from near as from far".  Fractals may be exactly the same at every
scale. They may be nearly the same at different scales. The concept of fractal extends beyond
trivial self-similarity and includes the idea of a detailed pattern repeating itself!
A fractal is a shape that if repeated over and over will create the illusion of a
three dimensional landscape.  Fractals are used in CGI “Computer Generated
Images” that have become common

 Wind tunnel analogy:  Small models are used to predict larger outcomes.
This smaller to larger correspondence is
not unlike the construction of a model, to
determine how a larger building or
landscape might operate under
proportionately measured forces.  During
the design of a new airplane, a model
airplane is tested in a wind tunnel to
determine how it will behave under a
variety of conditions.  These aeronautical
test results will be used in the
construction of an actual airplane, whose
real time performance is expected to
correlate closely with the model.
 Thus Secondary Progressions are analogous to this wind tunnel model, in that they
represent a smaller unit or model of time that will be duplicated in real time situations.

Calculating Secondary Progressions
Secondary Progressions are relatively easy to calculate.  First, you can just count
ahead in your Ephemeris 1 day for each year of the person’s life.  This will give you
the “approximate” positions of the progressed planets.
However, to exactly calculate the secondary progressions you have to know: “When
are these positions listed in the Ephemeris exact?”  To determine the precise positions of the
secondary progressions you have to find what is called the ACD, or Adjusted Calculated Date.
To find the ACD calculate the GMT or UT of the person in question.
For our example, use the birth chart of Brad Pitt, born December 18, 1963 at
6:31am in Shawnee, Oklahoma, USA.
1). Brad’s birth time is 6:31am CST (Zone 6 West), thus his GMT or UT is 12:31pm
2). Find the number of hours from the UT or GMT to the following midnight.  The
number of hours to the following midnight is 11hours and 29minutes.
3). Whatever the number of hours, add this to the Sidereal Time listed in your Ephemeris at midnight on
the date of birth.
Use a midnight Ephemeris. (All of you have a Midnight Ephemeris)

4). Using your Ephemeris for the year 1963 count forward if the GMT at birth is PM, or backwards if the
GMT is AM at birth, and find the date in your Ephemeris that is closest  to the Sid: Time of 17:12:33.  The
Ephemeris Sid. Time is 17:13:30.
You will find that this date is June 10.
5). June 10 is your ACD or Adjusted Calculated Date.  This means that all of the positions listed in your
Ephemeris for 1964 will correspond to June 10 of the year in question.
6). For example, if you count in your Ephemeris from December 18, 1963 to December 19, 1963, then
the positions listed in the Ephemeris for December 19, 1963 correspond to the actual date of June 10,
1964.
7). As another example, suppose we wanted to know Brad Pitt’s secondary progressions for his 20th year?
Count ahead, starting with December 18, 1963 as the zero (0) date, you would find that January 7
corresponds to 20.  Thus the positions listed in your Ephemeris for January 7, 1964 correspond to June
10, 1983. However, on June 10, 1983, Brad Pitt is still 19 years old.  For the progressed positions that
occur after he turns 20 years old you’d have to look at January 8, 1964 – this would correspond to June
10, 1984, so he would have already turned 20.  His exact positions for Dec. 19, 1983 would be different.
