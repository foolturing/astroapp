Career analysis Case from students
For Mar. 8th
1) Sex: Female
2) Birth Time: Oct.26, 1989; 09:40 a.m.
3) Birth Place: Luzhou, China (28N54, 105E27 on Kepler)
4) Current Occupation:
a)
I am a Credit and loan manager in a bank. The customers are companies. This
job involves marketing to the customers and detailed investigation of those
companies’ situation. However, I don’t quite like to get involved in
marketing. I prefer to do the detailed investigation, write reports and initiate
loans after my colleagues finish the marketing process.
5) Past Occupation:
a)
I used to be an auditor in KPMG. I had little time to stay at home due to
longtime working overtime and frequent business trips. And the longer you
stay in the “Big Four” accounting firms, the harder it would be for you to
look for an ideal job. Therefore, I quitted this job two years later.
6) Recent confusion at the career development:
a)
I always find it’s really difficult to realize my value. I do much more work
than others in my company, but I only get very limited reward. Other
colleagues think that I’m capable and good-tempered, so they would ask
me to do almost anything. I feel really bad for this.
b) My work involves marketing, but I don’t like marketing. I prefer detailed
paperwork. That’s why I’m wondering if such a job fits me or not.
c)
I have broad interests. I began to dance when I was a kid. I love baking and
making things by hand. I’m not sure if I can set up a studio to do what I like
to do in the future. Or when is the appropriate time for me to quit the job
that people consider as formal to do what I like to do?
