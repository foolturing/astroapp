Sometimes people will say I seem to be hiding something, or that I seem like I’m
“up to something,” but I’m not up to anything for the most part.
3). Circle the following most indicative of your usual facade or surface attitude?
a. Communicative
b. quiet
c. assertive
d. receptive
I can “get to talking” but I’m usually b. quiet and d. receptive.
Others

Crazy folks.  I don’t know?  People from all walks of life, though most people
would say “low life.”  You know, my closest friends tend to be mavericks and
sometimes dangerous types.

People that are there for you in a crisis, that don’t judge you.

It’s changed a lot.  I would say acceptance, hope – someone that believes in me

Home
9).  Briefly describe what your home life was like growing up?
It was upscale, we had money and stuff.  Pretty normal for uppity folks families I
guess.
10.) How did you respond to the conditions of your home life growing up?
I don’t remember much until I was 7 or 8.  I’m told I was a sweet little kid, easy
going.  My older sister fought with my Mom; they did not get along.
11.) What kind of man was your father when you were growing up?
My biological father committed suicide when I was five.  I have only a few vague
memories of him.  My step-father came along when I was around eight; he
was/is a good man – very successful.  He was gone a lot on cases, traveling, etc.
12.) What was your father’s career?
My biological father was a real estate attorney and had his own real estate
company.  He made good money I suppose.  I’m told he was a good salesman
and like to buy stuff.  My stepfather is a malpractice attorney, highly successful
– a “big shot” – he was President of the Bar Association for years.
13.) What was your mother like when you were growing up?
My mother was a go-getter, ambitious, hard working.  She was a real no
nonsense kind of person, you always knew where you stood with her.  If she
was unhappy you knew it, but she could be encouraging too.  She pushed me to
be successful.
