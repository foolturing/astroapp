Isn’t it easier to find aspects just by looking at the signs?  All signs
of like elements i.e. fire, earth, air and water signs are trine each
other, right?  And all signs of like modality meaning cardinal,
fixed or mutable signs are either square or opposite, correct?
Answer: In the natural zodiac this is true, and it is very often true
in an actual chart – which can be “helpful” in finding aspects.  However, aspects
are measured by “degree,” and you can have what are called “out of sign”
aspects.  Therefore finding aspects by sign only could be very misleading.  For
example, Mars in the sign of Capricorn could actually trine Jupiter in Aries, two
signs that are square each other in the natural zodiac.  Suppose natal Mars was
at 2 degrees of Capricorn and natal Jupiter was at 28 degrees of Aries, what is
exact angle between them?
2:        compared to        28:               What is the aspect angle?
If Mars was at 28 degrees of Capricorn we would have an exact square, as Aries
and Capricorn are square each other as signs in the natural zodiac.  However,
Mars is at only 2 degrees, which is 26 degrees before such an exact square.
Then how do you find the angle between Mars and Jupiter?
90: + 26 = 116:  Is this within the 7 degree orb of a trine?  “Yes!”  The
trine in this case is 4: from an exact trine of 120: ,well within the orb of 7:.
This is called an OUT OF SIGN TRINE
An “out of sign trine” represents greater
ease between two signs that generally
challenge each other.  Think of this as being
like two people that are often conflicted
finding themselves more at ease with each
other.  Though an out of sign trine may not
express as purely as an in-sign trine, it still
shows the ability to harmonize two very
differently motivated energies.  What might otherwise represent a dilemma,
now finds easier expression.  Let’s break this aspect down into its components
to understand what this means.
